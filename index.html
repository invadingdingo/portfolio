my work

beatZONE

beatZONE is a (successful!) attempt at something rhythm based. I loved the idea of a boss fight where the bosses attacks are timed to the music in the game, so taking that idea, and the theme for the jam (limited), me and a friend developed beatZONE. We implemented the theme by only allowing the player to shoot while in a designated zone. beatZONE was developed for Bullet Hell Jam 2023 in a week, scoring 29th place overall.

  • Unity
  • C#
  • Ableton

Difference

Difference is a fun take on the classic spot-the-difference genre. I developed it shortly after playing Inscryption, and really wanted to recreate the movement in that game (3D grid-based). The game is largely unfinished, but the movement mechanic works flawlessly! Unfortunately, I began developing this right around when I started working a new job, so it fell to the wayside. That said, I am very proud of the progress I made on it and solved a fun problem while making it.

  • Unity
  • C#
  • A LOT of 3D math.

about me

You've seen some of my work, so I guess I should introduce myself. I'm Samuel Everett. I've been making games ever since I could remember. Most were not good. But, you win some, you lose some. I knew from middle school that I wanted to do something with computers, but I wanted my work to matter. I want to make games that people can express emotions with, games that will find a place in the heart of the player. Unfortunately, writing isn't one of my strong suits. Instead, I stick with creating mechanics. I find endless joy in creating inventive and (hopefully!) fun mechanics that the player won't tire of after the thousandth time they use it. In addition to that, I have pretty extensive knowledge of 3D modeling, animation, and music creation. I did not show any of those skills here, but if you want to see them, totally feel free to reach out!
